Logging as an Interface

February 27, 2026 · Operability
Posts Books about

Logs are not exhaust. They are an API for your future self.

A good log line answers three questions: what happened, who it affected, and what to do next. If any of those is missing, the line is noise. If all three are present, the line is a tool.

Structured logs with short human summaries work best in practice. The summary is for paging. The fields are for debugging. This split keeps incidents quick and postmortems precise.
