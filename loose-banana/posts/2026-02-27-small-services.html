The Quiet Power of Small Services

February 27, 2026 · Architecture
Posts Books about

When a service fits in your head, you ship faster and break less. That is the whole story.

Small services win because they make reasoning cheap. You can predict blast radius, localize failures, and keep deployment boring. When a change turns risky, it is often because the boundary drifted, not because the code is hard.

I keep a simple rule: if a service needs a new explanation every time I open it, it is too big. Split it or trim it until the default mental model is enough.

The punchline is not microservices. It is proportion. Keep interfaces explicit, keep state narrow, and keep ownership clear.
